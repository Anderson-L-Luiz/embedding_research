The movie in question is "Saving Private Ryan," directed by Steven Spielberg. It's a film about a family of four brothers, three of whom are killed during World War II, and the government's decision to send a team to find and rescue the fourth brother, Private Ryan. The movie is not a glorification of war but a realistic and violent portrayal of its effects, with a focus on saving one member of a family. It's set in the summer of 1944 and features a non-heroic protagonist, Captain John Miller, who dies at the end. The film is known for its intense and realistic depiction of war, as well as its use of 100 cameras to capture the scenes. It won five Oscar prizes, including Best Director for Spielberg.
